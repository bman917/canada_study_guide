# Canadian Symbols

## The Canadian Crown

Canada has maintained constitutional monarchy status since Confederation in 1867.

The Crown symbolizes government institutions including:
- Parliament
- Legislatures
- Courts
- Police
- Canadian Forces

---

## Flags in Canada

### The National Flag (1965)

- Features a red-white-red pattern derived from the Royal Military College flag
- The maple leaf is the central element

### Historical

- The **Canadian Red Ensign** served as the national flag for approximately a century before 1965

### Official Colors

- **Red and white** have represented Canada's official colors since 1921

### Union Jack

- Remains the official Royal Flag of Canada

---

## National Symbols

### The Maple Leaf

Canada's most recognizable symbol:
- Adopted by French-Canadians in the 1700s
- Appears on military insignia since the 1850s

### The Fleur-de-lys

- Historical French royal symbol
- Incorporated into the Canadian Red Ensign
- Adopted by Quebec on its provincial flag in 1948

### Coat of Arms and Motto

- Adopted post-World War I
- Features **"A Mari Usque Ad Mare"** (From sea to sea)
- Incorporates symbols representing England, France, Scotland, and Ireland

### The Beaver

- Adopted centuries ago as a Hudson's Bay Company symbol
- Appears on the five-cent coin
- Featured on provincial coats of arms

## Parliament Buildings

- **Gothic Revival** structures completed in the 1860s
- **Centre Block** was rebuilt in 1922 after a 1916 fire
- **Peace Tower** added in 1927 to commemorate World War I

## Official Languages

English and French are constitutionally official.

### Official Languages Act (1969)

Established three objectives:
1. Ensure equality between languages in government
2. Maintain minority language communities
3. Promote societal equality

---

## National Anthem

**"O Canada"** became the official anthem in **1980**.

There are distinct French and English versions.

---

## Honours System

### Order of Canada

- Established in **1967** during Canada's centennial
- Canada's highest civilian honour

### Victoria Cross

- Highest honour awarded for exceptional bravery
- **96 Canadians** have received it since 1854
