## Memorable Quotes

### Sir Wilfrid Laurier
*7th Prime Minister of Canada (1896-1911)*

> "For here [in Canada], I want the marble to remain the marble; the granite to remain the granite; the oak to remain the oak; and out of these elements, I would build a nation great among the nations of the world."

### John Diefenbaker
*13th Prime Minister of Canada (1957-1963)*

> "I am a Canadian, a free Canadian, free to speak without fear, free to worship in my own way, free to stand for what I think right, free to oppose what I believe wrong, or free to choose those who shall govern my country. This heritage of freedom I pledge to uphold for myself and all mankind."

## Authorities

### Section 5 of the Citizenship Act

The Minister must grant citizenship to applicants who have "an adequate knowledge of Canada and the responsibilities and privileges of citizenship."

### Section 15 of the Citizenship Regulations

**Knowledge of Canada Requirements:**

Applicants must demonstrate understanding of:
- National symbols of Canada
- Chief characteristics of Canadian political and military history
- Chief characteristics of Canadian social and cultural history
- Chief characteristics of Canadian physical and political geography
- Canadian system of government as a constitutional monarchy
- Other Canada-related characteristics

**Knowledge of Citizenship Responsibilities and Privileges:**

Applicants must demonstrate understanding of:
- Participation in Canadian democratic processes
- Participation in Canadian society, including volunteerism, respect for the environment
- Rights, freedoms, and legal obligations
- Other citizenship responsibilities and privileges
