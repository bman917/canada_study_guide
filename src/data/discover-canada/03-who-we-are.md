# Who We Are

## Canada's Constitutional Foundation

Canada maintains the oldest continuous constitutional tradition in the world and is the only constitutional monarchy in North America. The nation's institutions reflect the principle of "Peace, Order, and Good Government" from the 1867 British North America Act.

## The Three Founding Peoples

### Aboriginal Peoples

Ancestors of Aboriginal peoples migrated from Asia thousands of years ago. Aboriginal and treaty rights are constitutionally protected.

**Historical Context:**
- **Royal Proclamation of 1763:** Britain's King George III guaranteed Aboriginal territorial rights, forming the basis of modern treaties
- **Residential Schools (1800s-1980s):** Inflicted hardship on Aboriginal children through forced assimilation; Ottawa formally apologized in 2008

### English Canadians

- Approximately 18 million Anglophones
- English is one of two official languages

### French Canadians

- Approximately 7 million Francophones
- French is one of two official languages

**Acadians:** French colonist descendants who settled in the Maritimes beginning in 1604. They survived the "Great Upheaval" deportations (1755-1763) and maintained their identity despite persecution.

**Quebecers:** Majority French-speaking population; recognized as "a nation within a united Canada" (2006).

**Anglo-Quebecers:** 1 million residents with 250-year heritage.

## Modern Canadian Diversity

The majority of Canadians were born in this country historically, yet Canada functions as a land of immigrants with millions contributing over 200 years.

### Religious Composition

- Christianity predominates (Catholic is the largest denomination)
- Growing Muslim, Jewish, Hindu, Sikh, and non-religious populations
- State partners with faith communities on social welfare and refugee resettlement

### Immigrant Groups

Largest ethnic communities include:
- English
- French
- Scottish
- Irish
- German
- Italian
- Chinese
- Ukrainian
- Dutch
- South Asian
- Scandinavian populations

Since the 1970s, most immigrants originate from Asian countries.

### Language Diversity

Chinese languages rank second after English in major cities:
- 13% in Vancouver speak Chinese at home
- 7% in Toronto speak Chinese at home

### LGBTQ+ Rights

Gay and lesbian Canadians enjoy the full protection of and equal treatment under the law, including access to civil marriage.

## Canadian Achievement: Marjorie Turner-Bailey

**Marjorie Turner-Bailey**, a descendant of black Loyalists and escaped slaves who came to Canada in the 1780s, became an Olympian and a symbol of Canadian diversity and achievement.

## Expectations for Newcomers

Newcomers are expected to embrace democratic principles such as the rule of law and should not import violent, extreme or hateful prejudices.
