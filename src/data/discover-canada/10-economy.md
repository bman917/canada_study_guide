# Canada's Economy

## A Trading Nation

Canada maintains its standard of living through international commerce.

### Free Trade Agreements

- **1988:** Canada enacted free trade with the United States
- **1994:** Expanded to include Mexico through **NAFTA** (North American Free Trade Agreement)
- **2020:** NAFTA replaced by **USMCA** (United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement)
- The agreement encompasses over **444 million people**
- Exceeded **$1 trillion** in merchandise trade by 2008

### Global Standing

- Canada ranks among the **world's ten largest economies**
- Participates in the **G7 group** (formerly G8) alongside:
  - United States
  - Germany
  - United Kingdom
  - Italy
  - France
  - Japan
  - (Russia was suspended in 2014)

---

## Three Main Economic Sectors

### 1. Service Industries

**Over 75%** of Canadian workers are employed in services, including:
- Transportation
- Education
- Healthcare
- Construction
- Banking
- Communications
- Retail
- Tourism
- Government

### 2. Manufacturing Industries

Canadian manufacturers produce:
- Paper
- High-tech equipment
- Aerospace technology
- Automobiles
- Machinery
- Food
- Clothing

Products serve both domestic and international markets. The **United States** represents the largest international trading partner.

### 3. Natural Resources Industries

This sector encompasses:
- Forestry
- Fishing
- Agriculture
- Mining
- Energy

Historically central to Canada's development, natural resource commodities comprise a significant portion of Canadian exports.

---

## Canada-U.S. Trade Relationship

The bilateral relationship represents the **biggest bilateral trading relationship in the world**.

### Canadian Exports to the U.S.

Over **three-quarters** of Canadian exports target the U.S. market, including:
- Energy products
- Industrial goods
- Machinery and equipment
- Automotive products
- Agricultural items
- Consumer goods

### Symbol of Partnership

The **Peace Arch** at Blaine, Washington symbolizes this partnership with inscriptions representing unity between the two nations.
