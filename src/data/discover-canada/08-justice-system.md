# The Justice System

## Overview

The Canadian judicial framework ensures procedural fairness for all citizens.

**Fundamental Principle:** Everyone is innocent until proven guilty.

The system rests on:
- Rule of law
- Freedom under legal structures
- Democratic governance
- Due process protections

---

## Core Legal Foundation

Due process requires government respect for all lawful entitlements. Laws represent written societal guidelines:
- Created by elected officials
- Enforced by police
- Interpreted by courts

The legal framework applies universally: to ordinary citizens, judicial officials, and law enforcement alike.

### Purpose of Legislation

- Maintaining social order
- Providing dispute resolution mechanisms
- Reflecting Canadian values

---

## Court System Structure

### Supreme Court of Canada

The **highest judicial authority** in Canada.

### Federal Court of Canada

Addresses matters involving federal government jurisdiction.

### Provincial Courts

Most provinces contain:

| Court Type | Function |
|------------|----------|
| **Appeal Courts** | Hear appeals from lower courts |
| **Trial Courts** | Court of Queen's Bench or Supreme Court (varies by province) |
| **Provincial Courts** | Minor infractions |
| **Family Courts** | Family law matters |
| **Traffic Courts** | Traffic violations |
| **Small Claims Courts** | Civil disputes involving limited monetary amounts |

---

## Law Enforcement

### Police Functions

Police maintain public safety and enforce legal statutes.

Citizens may request police assistance for:
- Traffic incidents
- Theft
- Assault situations
- Crime witnesses
- Missing persons

### Police Organizations

| Organization | Jurisdiction |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Provincial Forces** | Ontario and Quebec maintain dedicated provincial police |
| **Municipal Departments** | All provinces have municipal police services |
| **RCMP (Royal Canadian Mounted Police)** | Enforces federal legislation nationwide; provides provincial policing (except Ontario and Quebec) and municipal services in select areas |

### Your Right to Question Police

Citizens retain the right to question police conduct through established complaint procedures available in most Canadian jurisdictions.

---

## Legal Assistance Resources

Attorneys provide representation and legal counsel.

For financial constraints, most communities offer **legal aid services**: either free or at reduced cost.
