# Rights and Responsibilities of Citizenship

## Historical Foundation

Canadian rights derive from multiple legal traditions. Canada has an 800-year old tradition of ordered liberty dating back to the **Magna Carta (1215)**, which established foundational freedoms.

---

## Rights of Canadian Citizens

### Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms (1982)

The Charter enshrines fundamental protections and recognizes principles that recognize the supremacy of God and the rule of law.

### Fundamental Freedoms

Canadians enjoy the following fundamental freedoms:
- Freedom of conscience and religion
- Freedom of thought, belief, opinion, and expression, including freedom of speech and of the press
- Freedom of peaceful assembly
- Freedom of association

### Mobility Rights

Citizens may:
- Reside and work anywhere within Canada
- Travel freely internationally
- Obtain passports

### Aboriginal Peoples' Rights

The Charter ensures that rights do not diminish existing Indigenous treaty rights. Aboriginal and treaty rights are constitutionally protected.

### Language Rights

French and English hold equal status in Parliament and government operations. Both are official languages for federal services.

### Multiculturalism

Multiculturalism is described as a fundamental characteristic of the Canadian heritage and identity.

### Equality of Women and Men

Men and women are equal under the law. Canada does not extend protection to barbaric cultural practices including spousal abuse or forced marriage.

## Responsibilities of Canadian Citizens

### Legal and Social Obligations

1. **Obey the law**
   - The "rule of law" principle means individuals and governments alike are bound by law
   - No person or group is above the law

2. **Take responsibility for yourself and your family**
   - Employment, family care, and contributing according to abilities represent core Canadian values

3. **Serve on a jury**
   - Mandatory participation when summoned
   - Essential to maintaining impartial juries

4. **Vote in elections**
   - Participating in federal, provincial, territorial, and local elections

5. **Help others in the community**
   - Volunteering through food banks, schools, or supporting newcomer integration

6. **Protect and enjoy our heritage and environment**
   - Protecting natural, cultural and architectural heritage for future generations

## Defending Canada

Military service is **not compulsory** in Canada.

However, optional opportunities include:
- **Regular Canadian Forces:** Navy, army, and air force
- **Part-time reserves and militia**
- **Cadet programs for youth**
- **Coast Guard and emergency services:** Police and fire departments
