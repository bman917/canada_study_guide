# Modern Canada

## Trade and Economic Growth

The postwar period brought significant prosperity to Canada.

### Key Developments

- Trade liberalization through agreements like GATT (now WTO)
- Oil discovery in Alberta in 1947 launched the modern energy sector
- By 1951, most Canadians could afford adequate food, shelter, and clothing
- Strong economic growth between 1945-1970, particularly through U.S. trade relations

### Social Programs

- **Employment Insurance:** 1940
- **Old Age Security:** 1927
- **Canada/Quebec Pension Plans:** 1965
- **Canada Health Act:** Ensuring consistent healthcare coverage

---

## International Engagement

Canada developed autonomy gradually and made international contributions:

- Joined **NATO** during the Cold War
- Participated in **NORAD** with the United States
- Joined the **United Nations**
- Participated in the **Korean War** (1950-53)
- Engaged in numerous UN peacekeeping missions globally

---

## Canada and Quebec

French-Canadian culture flourished postwar.

### The Quiet Revolution (1960s)

Prompted constitutional changes including:

- **Royal Commission on Bilingualism and Biculturalism** (1963)
- **Official Languages Act** (1969) guaranteeing French and English services
- Canada helped found **La Francophonie** (1970)

### Sovereignty Referendums

Two referendums in Quebec on sovereignty were defeated:
- 1980
- 1995

---

## A Changing Society

Significant social transformation occurred over 50+ years:

- Expanded educational opportunities
- Women entered professional workforces
- **1948:** Voting rights extended to Japanese-Canadians
- **1960:** Voting rights extended to Aboriginal peoples
- Canada welcomed refugees from Communist countries:
  - ~37,000 Hungarians (1956)
  - 50,000+ Vietnamese
- Multiculturalism became central to Canadian identity
